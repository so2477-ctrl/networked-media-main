Galbijjim (Korean Braised Short Ribs)

  1. Soak the ribs in cold water for at least 1 hour (or up to overnight) to draw out excess blood, changing water a couple of times. * Drain and rinse. *Parboil (to remove impurities)
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  3. Add ribs and boil for 5 to 7 minutes. Skim off foam.
  4. Drain and rinse ribs under cold water.
  5. Make the sauce
    6. * In a bowl, combine soy sauce, sugar, honey, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, pepper, and 2 cups of water or beef broth. Cook the ribs
  6. In a large heavy pot or Dutch oven, place the parboiled ribs.
  7. Pour the sauce over them.
  8. Bring to a boil, then lower heat, cover, and simmer for about 1 hour, skimming fat as needed. Add vegetables
  9. Add radish, carrots, potatoes, onion, and shiitake mushrooms.
  10. Simmer for another 30 to 40 minutes, for extra tenderness and softness of the vegetables. * Add more water if needed.
  11. Final seasoning
  12. Taste and adjust with more soy sauce or sugar depending on your preference. * If you want a thicker sauce, simmer uncovered for the last 10 minutes.
  13. Finish and serve
    14. * Add green onions and optional garnishes (chestnuts, jujubes, pine nuts) just before serving. * Serve hot with steamed rice and kimchi

Soy Sauce Tteokbokki (Soy Sauce Rice Cakes)

  1. Prepare rice cakes * If refrigerated or frozen, soak tteok in warm water for 10 to 15 minutes until softened. Drain.
  2. 2. Make broth base * In a wide pan, add 1 and a half cups of water (or anchovy broth for richer flavor). * Stir in soy sauce, sugar, garlic, and a pinch of pepper. Bring to a boil.
  3. Cook rice cakes * Add tteok to the pan and boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally so they do not stick. * Simmer until the rice cakes become soft and chewy (about 7 to 10 minutes).
  4. 4. Add vegetables and fish cakes * Stir in onion, carrot, and fish cakes. * Continue simmering until sauce slightly thickens (5 to 7 minutes).
  5. Finish * Drizzle sesame oil, toss in green onions, and cook for 1 more minute. * Garnish with sesame seeds before serving.

Tomato corn salad

  1. Prepare the base
  2. In a large bowl, combine corn, beans, tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, and cilantro.
  3. Make the dressing
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together olive oil, lime juice, honey, garlic, salt, pepper, and cumin until smooth.
  5. Combine
  6. Pour the dressing over the salad mixture. Toss gently until everything is evenly coated.
  7. Fold in avocado cubes right before serving to keep them from browning.
  8. Let the salad sit in the fridge for 20 to 30 minutes for flavors to meld. Serve cold as a side, in tacos, or over greens.
